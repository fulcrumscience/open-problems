opc-go-006

Functional Validation of Viral Type 2 IRES-Ribosome Interactions via Translation Assays

READY TO TEST Score 0.527 $8,500 – $15,000

Problem mapping

Problem: Lack of complementary biochemical validation for the structural findings of viral Type 2 IRES recruitment to ribosomal preinitiation complex

Sub-question: Do the structural observations correlate with functional translation activity in cellular or in vitro systems?

Objective

Correlate structural observations of IRES-ribosome contacts with functional translation activity using in vitro and cellular reporter assays with site-directed IRES mutants.

Readouts

  • Primary: Relative luciferase activity (firefly/renilla ratio) from bicistronic reporter constructs in mammalian cells, normalized to wild-type IRES
  • Secondary: In vitro translation efficiency measured by luciferase luminescence from rabbit reticulocyte lysate reactions with mutant vs wild-type IRES constructs
  • Secondary: Ribosome binding affinity (Kd values) determined by fluorescence polarization or biolayer interferometry for mutant vs wild-type IRES with 40S ribosomal subunits

Experimental design

Overview: Generate 8-12 site-directed IRES mutants targeting structurally-observed ribosome contact residues, then measure translation efficiency in parallel using dual-luciferase bicistronic reporters in HeLa cells and in vitro translation systems to establish structure-function relationships.

  • WP1: Design and generate IRES mutants (weeks 1-3) - identify 8-12 residues/domains from structural data making ribosome contacts, design mutations (alanine scanning, charge reversals, deletions), clone into bicistronic vectors (HCV-IRES, EMCV-IRES, or target Type 2 IRES) between renilla and firefly luciferase ORFs, sequence-verify all constructs
  • WP2: Cellular translation assays (weeks 4-7) - transfect HeLa and HEK293T cells with wild-type and mutant bicistronic reporters, harvest at 24h and 48h, measure dual-luciferase activity, perform dose-response with 3 plasmid concentrations, include cap-dependent translation control constructs
  • WP3: In vitro translation and binding assays (weeks 6-10) - transcribe IRES-firefly constructs in vitro, perform translation in rabbit reticulocyte lysate and wheat germ extract systems, measure time-course luciferase activity, purify 40S subunits from HeLa cells, perform fluorescence polarization binding assays with fluorescein-labeled IRES RNAs
  • WP4: Data integration and validation (weeks 11-12) - statistical analysis of all translation data, correlation analysis between structural contact distances and functional defects, Western blotting to confirm equal protein loading in cellular assays, RNA stability assays (Northern blot or RT-qPCR) to exclude RNA folding defects

Controls:

  • Positive control: Wild-type IRES sequence in bicistronic reporter, expected firefly/renilla ratio of 0.5-2.0 depending on IRES type
  • Negative control: Bicistronic construct with no IRES (empty intercistronic spacer), expected firefly/renilla ratio <0.05, validates no cap-dependent reinitiation or cryptic IRES activity
  • Cap-dependent control: Monocistronic firefly luciferase under CMV promoter to normalize for transfection efficiency and general translation capacity
  • RNA stability control: Mutations in non-contact regions predicted by structure to have no ribosome interaction, should maintain wild-type translation levels

Sample size plan: N=6 biological replicates per construct in cellular assays (3 independent transfections × 2 time points), N=4 technical replicates per construct in vitro translation assays, N=3 technical replicates for binding assays with triplicate measurements each

Success criteria:

  • At least 50% of mutations targeting direct ribosome contact residues (as identified by <5Å distance in structure) show >2-fold reduction in translation efficiency with p<0.01 vs wild-type
  • Correlation coefficient r>0.6 between structural contact surface area (Å²) and functional translation defect (fold-change) across all mutants
  • Mutations in non-contact control regions maintain >70% of wild-type translation activity, and RNA stability >80% of wild-type by Northern blot or RT-qPCR
  • In vitro and cellular assay results show concordance with Pearson r>0.5 for mutant phenotypes

Estimated timeline: 12 weeks

Lab handoff checklist

  • Structural coordinates (PDB file or CryoEM map) with annotated ribosome-IRES contact residues identified at <5Å, <8Å, and <10Å distance thresholds, including contact surface area calculations and list of 12-15 candidate residues for mutagenesis prioritized by contact type (base-specific, backbone, protein contacts)
  • Complete wild-type IRES sequence (nucleotides) with secondary structure annotation, domain boundaries, and functional elements identified in literature, formatted for direct cloning into pRF vector between renilla and firefly luciferase genes with appropriate restriction sites or Gibson assembly overhangs
  • Mutagenesis strategy document specifying: (1) 8-12 target positions with rationale based on structural contacts, (2) specific mutations for each position (e.g., single nucleotide changes, small deletions, charge reversals), (3) 2-3 negative control mutations in non-contact regions, (4) predicted impact on RNA secondary structure using RNAfold or similar tool
  • Cell culture protocols including validated transfection conditions for HeLa and HEK293T cells with the specific bicistronic vector system, optimal plasmid amounts, and harvest timepoints from preliminary experiments or literature
  • Data analysis template with pre-defined normalization methods (firefly/renilla ratio calculation, fold-change relative to wild-type, statistical tests for N=6 replicates), correlation analysis pipeline for structure-function relationships, and figure templates for publication
