opc-go-005

Transcriptomic Profiling of Pachytene-Specific Gene Activation in Meiotic Cells

READY TO TEST Score 0.527 $18,000 – $25,000

Problem mapping

Problem: The concept of 'pachytene genome activation' (PGS) is introduced without proper characterization of which genes are activated, what defines this process, or what mechanisms underlie it

Sub-question: What is the complete set of genes that are specifically upregulated during pachytene stage (pachytene genome activation)?

Objective

To identify and validate the complete set of genes specifically upregulated during the pachytene stage of meiosis through temporal RNA-seq profiling across meiotic progression.

Readouts

  • Primary: Differential gene expression profiles across meiotic stages (leptotene, zygotene, pachytene, diplotene) identifying pachytene-upregulated genes (fold-change ≥2, FDR<0.05)
  • Secondary: RT-qPCR validation of top 20 pachytene-enriched candidates showing stage-specific expression patterns
  • Secondary: RNA in situ hybridization of 5 representative pachytene-specific genes confirming cellular localization and temporal expression

Experimental design

Overview: Purify staged meiotic spermatocytes from mouse testes using FACS-based separation (DNA content and surface markers), perform RNA-seq on biological quadruplicates per stage, and validate candidates through orthogonal methods.

  • WP1: Optimize and perform FACS-based isolation of meiotic stages (leptotene, zygotene, pachytene, diplotene) from juvenile mouse testes using Hoechst/surface marker staining; quality control by cytology and stage-specific marker RT-qPCR
  • WP2: Extract RNA, perform quality control (RIN>8), prepare libraries and sequence (150bp paired-end, 50M reads/sample) for 4 biological replicates per meiotic stage plus pre-meiotic spermatogonia control
  • WP3: Validate top 20 pachytene-enriched genes by RT-qPCR across all stages (technical triplicates) and perform RNA-FISH on testis sections for 5 representative genes across 3 biological replicates
  • WP4: Bioinformatic analysis including read alignment, differential expression (DESeq2), clustering, Gene Ontology enrichment, and motif analysis of pachytene-specific promoters

Controls:

  • Positive control: Known pachytene-expressed genes (e.g., Spo11, Dmc1, Sycp1) should show expected expression patterns
  • Negative control: Pre-meiotic spermatogonia sample to establish baseline and identify meiosis-specific vs pachytene-specific genes
  • Technical control: ERCC spike-in controls for RNA-seq normalization and batch effect assessment across libraries

Sample size plan: 4 biological replicates per stage (5 stages total = 20 RNA-seq samples); RT-qPCR validation on 3 independent biological replicates with 3 technical replicates each; RNA-FISH on 3 biological replicate animals with 10 sections per animal

Success criteria:

  • Identify ≥100 genes with pachytene-specific upregulation (≥2-fold vs all other stages, FDR<0.05) with clear stage separation in PCA analysis
  • RT-qPCR validation confirms pachytene enrichment for ≥80% of tested candidates (≥1.5-fold vs adjacent stages, p<0.05)
  • RNA-FISH demonstrates pachytene-stage localized expression for ≥4 of 5 tested genes with signal in >70% of pachytene cells

Estimated timeline: 16 weeks

Lab handoff checklist

  • Detailed FACS gating strategy document with representative plots showing separation of leptotene, zygotene, pachytene, and diplotene populations based on DNA content and forward/side scatter parameters
  • Complete list of validated primer sequences for 10 known stage-specific marker genes (2-3 per stage) to be used for quality control of sorted populations prior to RNA-seq
  • Bioinformatics pipeline specification including: reference genome version (e.g., mm10/GRCm38), alignment software (STAR v2.7+), differential expression package (DESeq2), cutoffs for defining pachytene-specific genes (statistical and fold-change thresholds), and clustering approach for temporal pattern analysis
  • Animal breeding schedule to ensure availability of 4 cohorts of day 18-20 juvenile mice (minimum 6 males per cohort) at 2-week intervals for biological replicates
