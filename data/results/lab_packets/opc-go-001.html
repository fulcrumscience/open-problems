opc-go-001

Edit Transformation-Competence Determinants in Recalcitrant Bioenergy Crop Genotypes

READY TO TEST Score 0.527 $12,000 – $28,000

Problem mapping

Problem: Genotype-specific transformation efficiency differences are driven by unknown underlying genetic or epigenetic variation, preventing widespread adoption of plant engineering in non-elite lines.

Sub-question: Can identified genetic or epigenetic determinants of transformation competence be edited to convert recalcitrant elite lines into transformable lines?

Objective

Test whether targeted editing of candidate competence loci increases Agrobacterium-mediated transformation efficiency in recalcitrant lines.

Readouts

  • Primary: stable transformation efficiency = PCR-positive independent events / immature embryos treated
  • Secondary: event quality (copy number, backbone integration, edit genotype at target loci)
  • Secondary: regeneration rate and fertility in T0/T1

Experimental design

Overview: Parallel construct and transformation study across one transformable control genotype and two recalcitrant genotypes, with multiplex CRISPR edits to top-ranked candidate loci.

  • WP1 candidate selection: choose 8-12 loci from prior omics/QTL literature and internal pilot data
  • WP2 construct build: 3-4 multiplex CRISPR constructs plus non-targeting control
  • WP3 Agrobacterium transformation: immature embryos or embryogenic callus using a validated sorghum/switchgrass protocol
  • WP4 selection and regeneration under hygromycin
  • WP5 molecular QC: targeted amplicon sequencing, transgene PCR/qPCR copy number, backbone check
  • WP6 statistics: mixed-effects model of transformation efficiency by genotype and construct

Controls:

  • Transformable genotype positive process control (e.g., TX430-like line)
  • Recalcitrant genotype + empty/non-targeting construct negative control
  • No-Agrobacterium contamination control on selection media

Sample size plan: Per construct per genotype: 120 immature embryos, 3 biological replicates (total ~1080 embryos for 3 genotypes x 3 constructs x 120).

Success criteria:

  • >=2x increase in stable transformation efficiency in >=1 recalcitrant genotype vs matched non-targeting control
  • No major regeneration penalty (>25% drop) compared with control
  • At least 30% of positive events show intended edit at one or more target loci

Estimated timeline: 20 weeks
